M89

An almost perfectly circular elliptical galaxy, M89 was discovered by Charles Messier in 1781. It contains a large number of globular clusters that cause an “extended envelope”, or larger field of light surrounding it. M89 is also home to a supermassive black hole.

Telescopes 8” and larger will resolve a faint patch.
