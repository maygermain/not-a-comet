M16

Eagle Nebula

Also known as the Eagle Nebula, M16 was discovered by Swiss astronomer Jean-Philippe Loys de Chéseaux in 1745. It is home to the Pillars of Creation – an active star-forming region in the nebula.

A small telescope will show the Eagle Nebula, but larger telescopes are needed to see the Pillars of Creation.
