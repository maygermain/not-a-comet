M48

M48 was discovered by Charles Messier in 1771.

It is visible with the naked eye in good viewing conditions, but instruments like binoculars or telescopes will resolve more details.

M48 is one of the so-called “lost” Messier objects – its identity has been debated, but it is currently identified as NGC 2548 – the only object in the vicinity that matches Messier’s description.
