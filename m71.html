M71 was discovered by the Swiss astronomer Jean-Philippe Loys de Chéseaux in 1746. While it was originally considered an open cluster, it is now classified as a globular cluster, albeit an unusually loose and small one. It has a lower concentration of stars at the center than the average globular cluster, and its stars are also far younger.

M71 appears as a faint patch in binoculars.
