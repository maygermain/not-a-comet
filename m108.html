M108

M108 was discovered by French astronomer Pierre Méchain in 1781, but was only added to the catalogue in 1953. It is one of the largest and brightest members of the Ursa Major cluster, and is also part of the Virgo cluster of galaxies. M107 is host to a supermassive black hole at its center.

Small telescopes will show M108 as a faint patch, but larger telescopes show more detail. While M108 is best viewed in April, it is visible all year round in the Northern hemisphere.
