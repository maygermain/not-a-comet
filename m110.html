M110

M110 was discovered by Charles Messier in 1773. A small elliptical galaxy, M110 shows evidence of young stars at the center, despite general consensus that elliptical galaxies are “dead” and devoid of star formation. It is a satellite of the Andromeda galaxy, M31.

Small telescopes show M110 as a faint patch, while larger telescopes will show an oval with a bright core. M110 is visible near the core of the Andromeda galaxy.
