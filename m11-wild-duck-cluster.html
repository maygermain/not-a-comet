M11

Wild Duck Cluster

Also known as the Wild Duck Cluster, M11 was discovered by the German astronomer Gottfried Kirch in 1681.

It is the farthest open cluster in the catalogue that can be seen with the naked eye; with a pair of binoculars, it appears as a triangular patch of light.
